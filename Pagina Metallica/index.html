ABOUT OF METALLICA

Metallica is an American thrash metal band founded in 1981 in Los Angeles by Lars Ulrich and James Hetfield. Over the years, they had several lineup changes, including the replacements of Dave Mustaine and Ron McGovney with Kirk Hammett and Cliff Burton. The band relocated to San Francisco after connecting with Cliff Burton. Tragically, Cliff Burton passed away in a bus accident in Sweden in 1986, leading to Jason Newsted's entry into the group. Later, he was succeeded by the current bassist, Robert Trujillo. As of their latest album release, "72 Seasons" on April 14, 2023, Metallica has put out 11 studio albums. Their total sales have surpassed 90 million records. They are recognized as one of the "big four" of thrash metal, along with Megadeth, Slayer, and Anthrax. The band has also earned numerous awards, including nine Grammys, MTV awards, American Music Awards, Billboard awards, and induction into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 2009. They have a star on the Kerrang! Walk of Fame.
